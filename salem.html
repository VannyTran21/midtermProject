Personal Review

Salem Witch Museum is the place if you want to learn more about the witch trials that happened way back in 1692. The museum is open all year around and offers life-size stage sets about the trials almost everyday. It taught me a lot about when and why witch trials started and the whole process wasn’t boring at all. You start with listening to the story of the history of witch trials (which is based on true story and real people) in a closed dark room and along with the storytelling is the stage effect that makes it even more lively and somewhat haunting. Then after that, there will be a tour guide that walks you through the hallways to introduce you to some of the “witch craft” that were used back in the day. At the end of the tour is a souvenir shop where they have a coin press machine that you can choose from 4 different designs of the museum to press from.

Where and When

  • Address: 19 1/2 North Washington Square, Washington Square E, Salem, MA 01970
  • Hours: everyday from 10am-5pm
